Santa Catalina Monastery, Arequipa, Peru

In the laid-back southern Peruvian colonial city of Arequipa, itself one of the most underrated cities of South America, is a stunning 16th-century monastery of Dominican nuns. Constructed from nearby volcanic rocks and painted in vibrant shades of blue and red, its narrow cloisters and lovely courtyards have come to resemble a city secluded within a city. Even the tourists wander silently so as to not break the spell of quiet contemplation. They say that peace has to come from within, but for those of us struggling with it otherwise, a sun-soaked afternoon here may be the next best thing.
